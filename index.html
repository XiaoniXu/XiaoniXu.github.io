About Me

I’m a second-year master’s student in biostatistics at Columbia University Mailman School of Public Health. My research focuses on causal mediation analysis, causal inference, and functional data analysis, particularly in health and biomedical applications.

Before Columbia, I earned a B.A. in Biology from the University of Chicago with a specification in Ecology and Evolution, where I investigated 3‑D morphometric evolution of avian cranial structures. I have collaborated with the Field Museum on projects integrating epidemiology, molecular biology, and machine learning.

Beyond statistics, I’m an avid birder. Sharing data‑driven insights with researchers and the public alike is at the heart of my work.
